  YES — with appropriate caveats:

  ► Strategy switching:
    • Trending Bull    → run momentum / trend-following strategies.
    • Trending Bear    → reduce equity exposure; rotate to bonds/cash.
    • Ranging/Sideways → mean-reversion strategies (pairs, stat-arb).
    • Crisis           → volatility-targeting, safe-haven allocation.

  ► Risk sizing:
    Annualised volatility per regime can feed directly into a
    volatility-targeting framework (e.g., target 10% ann. vol):
    position_size = target_vol / regime_ann_vol.

  ► Caveats:
    • Regimes are inferred with posterior uncertainty — always
      weight decisions by posterior probability, not point estimates.
    • Regime transitions are detected with a lag (minimum duration
      filter); act on confirmed transitions, not single-day signals.
    • The HMM is a generative model trained on historical data;
      future structural breaks (new regimes) may not be captured.
    • This output is research / educational — not financial advice.
